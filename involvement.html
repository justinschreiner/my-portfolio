My Involvement

Check out what I'm involved in...

SELF Fellowship

The SELF fellowship is a highly selective engineering fellowship at the University of Kansas. Through this program, I have been able to expand and refine my leadership, managerial, business, interpersonal, entrepreneurial, communication and engineering skills through mentoring, coursework, seminars and leadership opportunities.

SAP

I founded the Suicide Awareness and Prevention club at the University of Kansas my freshman year. Since that time, I have served as the club's president. The club hosts speaker events on campus which aim to educate the student body on warning signs of suicide. This role has allowed me to apply my leadership and managerial skills to make a difference in my community.

Vann Fellowship of Economics

The Vann Fellowship in Economics is a one year fellowship at the University of Kansas where members learn and apply critical economic concepts. This is done through coursework, lectures, and seminars from industry experts. I was selected to join this fellowship my sophomore year, and it allowed me to learn business skills which I would not have been exposed to through engineering curriculum.

Startup School

The Startup School at the University of Kansas was offered starting in 2020, and I signed up immediately. This course took place over several months, and it gave students interested in technology startups an inside look at starting, funding, growing, and operating a successful startup. I have always been extremely interested in being involved in a startup, so this opportunity was a great way to be introduced to this culture.

Sigma Phi Epsilon

Sigma Phi Epsilon is a fraternity at the University of Kansas which I have been a member of since my freshman year. This organization has provided me with opportunities to volunteer and improve my interpersonal skills.
